Why Neuroscience

I took my first plane ride before my first steps. Never in one country for more than 5 years, my whole life, I have been learning from the diversity this world has to offer. Through traveling, I recognized how despite differing healthcare systems and socioeconomic climates around the world, the stigma and helplessness associated with neurological disorders remained. Given the limited preventions and treatments for many neurological disabilities, society often unjustly marginalizes affected people. I believe I can best help redefine what many have deemed helpless by pursuing a PhD in Neuroscience.

Areas of Interest

I plan to use and further build on these computational foundations to help deepen our understanding of both biological and artificial neural networks and to develop brain machine interfaces and neural prosthetics. Living in a time where vast amounts and types of data are being generated, I am also interested in developing techniques to analyze this data, uncovering patterns and correlations which could answer some of neuroscience’s unanswered questions. A PhD’s commitment to scientific exploration both in terms of the breadth of topics being investigated and the flexibility offered to its students to choose the right laboratory aligns closely with my values and future goals.
